Wet Waste - Organic or Biodegradeable

Consists of: food scraps, fruit and vegetable peels, leftover food, egg shells, yard waste, tea bags. Does not consist of: milk packets/tetra packets, wrappers, empty boxes, containers. Keep a separate bin (ideally compostable) in the kitchen and ensure only biodegradable waste goes into it! Also, ensure the bin has drainage to prevent excess liquid buildup.

Dry Waste

Waste that does not decompose naturally and changes form only by applying heat or chemical reactions is known as non-biodegradable/inorganic/dry waste.
Recyclable: Paper, tissues, plastic bottles and containers, glass jars, metal cans.
Non-recyclable: Plastic wrappers, bags, thermal paper receipts, bubble wrap. Use two separate bins for recyclables and non-recyclables. Contact your nearby scrap dealer to dispose of them properly.

Electronic Waste

Electronic waste (e-waste) is a generic term for old, end-of-life, or discarded electrical and electronic equipment.
Examples: Computers, laptops, smartwatches, televisions, printers, refrigerators, washing machines, chargers, headphones.
Keep e-waste in designated areas, wipe personal data before disposal, and take it to certified e-waste recycling centers or manufacturer take-back programs.

Textile Waste

Textile waste includes discarded clothing, fabrics, and other textile materials. Recycling or repurposing these materials can reduce environmental impact.
